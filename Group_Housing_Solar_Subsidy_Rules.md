# MSEDCL Solar Subsidy Rules for Group Housing Societies (GHS) & RWAs

This document serves as a reference guide outlining the rules, eligibility, and subsidy structure for Group Housing Societies (GHS) and Resident Welfare Associations (RWA) adopting rooftop solar under the **PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana**, as implemented by MSEDCL in Maharashtra.

---

## 1. Subsidy Structure & Capacity Limits
Group Housing Societies and RWAs receive a fixed central subsidy for solar installations used to power common facilities (lifts, common lighting, water pumps, EV charging stations, etc.).

* **Subsidy Rate:** **₹18,000 per kW** of installed capacity.
* **Maximum Eligible Capacity:** Up to **500 kW** per society.
* **Per-Flat Limit:** The calculation of total eligible capacity is restricted to a maximum of **3 kW per house/flat** in the society.
* **Inclusions:** The 500 kW upper limit is inclusive of any individual rooftop plants already installed by individual residents in the GHS/RWA.

*Example:* A society installing a 100 kW system for common areas will receive a direct subsidy of ₹18,00,000 (100 kW × ₹18,000).

## 2. Eligibility & Equipment Mandates
To qualify for the subsidy, the society must strictly adhere to the government's equipment and vendor guidelines:
* **Approved Panels (ALMM):** Solar modules must be procured from the Approved List of Models and Manufacturers (ALMM).
* **Domestic Content Requirement (DCR):** Panels must be 100% domestically manufactured (Made in India). Imported panels **do not** qualify for the subsidy.
* **Empanelled Vendors:** Installation must be executed exclusively by solar vendors officially empanelled by MNRE/MSEDCL.
* **Net Metering:** The system must be connected to the grid via a Net Metering arrangement at the Single Point Delivery (SPD) common meter.

## 3. MSEDCL Specific Application Rules
As the implementing DISCOM in Maharashtra, MSEDCL mandates the following operational process:
* **Online Portal:** Applications must be submitted through the National Solar Portal (pmsuryaghar.gov.in) or MSEDCL's I-SMART consumer portal.
* **Application Processing Fees:** MSEDCL charges a processing fee of **₹500** for a sanctioned load up to 20 kW, plus **₹100** for every subsequent 20 kW (or part thereof).
* **Feasibility Approval:** MSEDCL will conduct a technical feasibility check, primarily assessing the Distribution Transformer (DTC) capacity before approving the installation.
* **Individual Flat Owners:** Individual apartment owners within a society can still apply separately for their own units (up to 3 kW) alongside the society’s common-area installation, provided they have legal rooftop rights and their own meter.

## 4. Disbursement Process (Direct Benefit Transfer)
* **Post-Installation:** Once the empanelled vendor installs the system, MSEDCL inspects the site and installs the bidirectional Net Meter.
* **Commissioning:** A commissioning certificate is issued upon successful grid connection.
* **Bank Credit:** The society must upload the commissioning certificate and a cancelled cheque on the portal. The subsidy is directly credited to the GHS/RWA’s bank account via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) within approximately **30 working days**.

## 5. System Warranties & Maintenance 
* **Performance Ratio (PR):** Under the empanelment guidelines, the Grid Connected Solar System must maintain a minimum Performance Ratio (PR) of **75%** throughout a mandatory 5-year warranty and Comprehensive Maintenance Contract (CMC) period.
* **Penalties:** Vendors failing to meet this 75% efficiency standard are liable to be penalized by MSEDCL (e.g., ₹1,000 per kW for each percentage shortfall), ensuring the society receives long-term, high-quality generation.
